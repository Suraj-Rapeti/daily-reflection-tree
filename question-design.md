# Question Design Workbook
## Daily Reflection Tree - Deep Design Phase

## AXIS 1: LOCUS OF CONTROL (Internal Agent vs Passive Observer)

### Opening Question (A1_OPEN)
**Goal:** Establish baseline perspective on the day
**Question:** "How would you describe today in one word?"
**Options:**
- Productive
- Mixed  
- Tough
- Frustrating

**Why these options?** Productive/Mixed suggest internal agency. Tough/Frustrating suggest external focus. Sets the frame for deeper Locus questions.

---

### Axis 1, Path A: Internal Locus Path (Follow-up question after Productive/Mixed)
**Question:** "You said it was {answer}. When something went well today, what made the difference?"
**Options:**
- A) "I prepared well / had clear thinking"  → INTERNAL
- B) "The team showed up / I got the right support"  → INTERNAL (attributed to others' internal, recognizes collaboration)
- C) "I got lucky / things broke my way"  → EXTERNAL
- D) "I adapted on the fly / found a workaround"  → INTERNAL (responsive agency)

**Why these options?**
- A is classic internal attribution
- B is interesting - internal but collaborative (not "I alone did this")
- C is external/luck
- D is reactive agency (not planning, but responding)

**Signal map:**
- A → axis1:internal
- B → axis1:internal  
- C → axis1:external
- D → axis1:internal

---

### Axis 1, Path B: External Locus Path (Follow-up question after Tough/Frustrating)
**Question:** "You said it was {answer}. When something didn't go as planned today, what did you do?"
**Options:**
- A) "I looked for what I could control / adjust"  → INTERNAL
- B) "I talked to someone who could help"  → INTERNAL (seeks support but takes agency)
- C) "I pushed through / kept doing my best"  → MIXED (effort but possibly stubborn)
- D) "I felt stuck / waited for the situation to change"  → EXTERNAL

**Why these options?**
- A is classic agency
- B shows growth mindset (seeks help to solve it)
- C is interesting - effort is good but can hide learned helplessness
- D is passive waiting (external)

**Signal map:**
- A → axis1:internal
- B → axis1:internal
- C → axis1:mixed
- D → axis1:external

---

### Axis 1 - Deepening Question (A1_DEEP) - Asked regardless of path
**Question:** "This one's harder: did you tell yourself a story today about why things happened the way they did? What was the story?"
**Options:**
- A) "I made it happen / I'm responsible"  → INTERNAL
- B) "We made it happen together"  → INTERNAL (collective agency)
- C) "It just happened / luck or timing"  → EXTERNAL
- D) "Others caused it / circumstances beyond me"  → EXTERNAL

**Why this question?**
- Narrative/story-telling reveals deep belief
- "What's the story you told yourself" is disarming - invites reflection
- Avoids blame-language while surfacing attribution

**Signal map:**
- A → axis1:internal
- B → axis1:internal
- C → axis1:external
- D → axis1:external

## AXIS 2: CONTRIBUTION ORIENTATION (Giver vs Taker / Citizen vs Entitled)

### Axis 2 Opening (A2_OPEN) - After bridge from Axis 1
**Question:** "Now shift gears. Think about what you gave today — and what you needed. Which resonates?"
**Options:**
- A) "I helped someone with something outside my role"  → CONTRIBUTION
- B) "I taught or mentored someone"  → CONTRIBUTION
- C) "I felt frustrated that I had to do others' work"  → ENTITLEMENT
- D) "I needed support that I didn't feel I got"  → ENTITLEMENT

**Why these options?**
- A & B are pure contribution (discretionary effort)
- C & D are entitlement-oriented (frustration about effort or recognition)
- Pairs contribution moment with entitlement feeling to avoid leading

**Signal map:**
- A → axis2:contribution
- B → axis2:contribution
- C → axis2:entitlement
- D → axis2:entitlement

---

### Axis 2 - Deepening Question (A2_DEEP)
**Question:** "When you did something extra or needed something today — what made you take that action?"
**Options:**
- A) "I saw someone needed help"  → CONTRIBUTION (empathy-driven)
- B) "It was the right thing to do"  → CONTRIBUTION (principle-driven)
- C) "I've earned the right to ask / deserve this"  → ENTITLEMENT
- D) "I was worried how things would look if I didn't"  → EXTERNAL (compliance, not genuine contribution)

**Why these options?**
- A & B are contribution (external focus - someone else)
- C is classic entitlement (earned right)
- D is interesting - effort motivated by fear/image, not genuine

**Signal map:**
- A → axis2:contribution
- B → axis2:contribution
- C → axis2:entitlement
- D → axis2:mixed (or could be external - neither true contribution nor entitlement)

---

### Axis 2 - Recognition Question (A2_RECOGNITION)
**Question:** "If you did something well today — did you need someone to notice?"
**Options:**
- A) "Yes, it would feel good if they noticed"  → CONTRIBUTION (recognition welcomed but not needed)
- B) "No — I knew it was right, that was enough"  → STRONG CONTRIBUTION
- C) "Yes, and I was frustrated they didn't"  → ENTITLEMENT
- D) "I expected to be recognized for my effort"  → ENTITLEMENT (expectation baked in)

**Why this question?**
- Entitlement reveals itself as resentment when recognition is absent
- Contribution reveals itself as internal satisfaction
- A vs B separates "gracious receiver" from "intrinsically motivated"
- C vs D separates "disappointment" from "expectation/demand"

**Signal map:**
- A → axis2:contribution
- B → axis2:contribution
- C → axis2:entitlement
- D → axis2:entitlement

## AXIS 3: RADIUS OF CONCERN (Self-Centric vs Team/Other-Centric)

### Axis 3 Opening (A3_OPEN) - After bridge from Axis 2
**Question:** "Let's zoom out. When you think about today's biggest moment or challenge — who comes to mind?"
**Options:**
- A) "Just me — it was my responsibility to solve"  → SELF-CENTRIC (narrow focus)
- B) "My team — we were all navigating it"  → TEAM-CENTRIC (widened radius)
- C) "A specific person who was struggling more"  → OTHER-CENTRIC (empathic radius)
- D) "The customer or person we serve"  → SYSTEMIC-CENTRIC (widest radius)

**Why these options?**
- Progresses from narrow (self) → wide (team) → wider (specific other) → widest (systemic)
- Not binary but a spectrum
- D is transcendent - thinking beyond org to end-user

**Signal map:**
- A → axis3:self-centric
- B → axis3:team-centric
- C → axis3:team-centric (shows perspective-taking / empathy)
- D → axis3:altrocentric

---

### Axis 3 - Perspective-Taking Question (A3_PERSPECTIVE)
**Question:** "When something went wrong today, did you wonder what it was like for someone else involved?"
**Options:**
- A) "I was focused on how it affected me / my work"  → SELF-CENTRIC
- B) "I wondered how my team was affected"  → TEAM-CENTRIC
- C) "I explicitly asked someone else how they were"  → ALTROCENTRIC (action, not just wondering)
- D) "I thought about the impact on the customer/end-user"  → ALTROCENTRIC (systemic empathy)

**Why this question?**
- Measures actual *perspective-taking* (Batson) not just feeling
- C adds behavioral component (asking)
- D adds systems-level thinking

**Signal map:**
- A → axis3:self-centric
- B → axis3:team-centric
- C → axis3:altrocentric
- D → axis3:altrocentric

---

### Axis 3 - Meaning Question (A3_MEANING)
**Question:** "What gave you the most meaningful moment in today?"
**Options:**
- A) "An achievement or win for me / my work"  → SELF-CENTRIC
- B) "A moment I solved something with the team"  → TEAM-CENTRIC
- C) "I helped someone get through something hard"  → ALTROCENTRIC (other's benefit was primary)
- D) "I realized I was part of something bigger"  → TRANSCENDENT (self-transcendence as Maslow defined it)

**Why this question?**
- Self-transcendence shows itself as "I suddenly felt part of something larger"
- Progression from personal win → team accomplishment → helping other → transcendence
- D is the key insight: meaning comes from recognizing one's place in a larger system

**Signal map:**
- A → axis3:self-centric
- B → axis3:team-centric
- C → axis3:altrocentric
- D → axis3:transcendent

## Summary Question (SUMMARY_RETROSPECT)
**Question (asked at end):** "Looking back at this conversation, what surprised you about your own answers?"
**Options:**
- A) "I saw more agency than I gave myself credit for"
- B) "I realized I give more than I think"
- C) "I think about others more than I expected to"
- D) "Nothing surprising — my answers felt aligned"

**Why this question?**
- Meta-reflection: does the person see the pattern emerging?
- Allows them to name the insight themselves
- Validates the journey through the tree

## Question Design Principles Applied

✓ **Specificity:** Questions point to concrete moments (not "Do you have agency?" but "What did you do when things didn't go as planned?")

✓ **Non-leading:** Options don't suggest right answers; they reflect real spectrum of human responses

✓ **Nested complexity:** Early questions broad, later ones probe deeper (after priming)

✓ **Interpolation ready:** All can reference earlier answers ("You said it was {A1_OPEN.answer}...")

✓ **No jargon:** These don't name the axes, they *surface* them through lived experience

✓ **Honest dichotomies:** Each pair of options is genuinely distinguishable (not false choices)
